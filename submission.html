SymposiumPlanner-2012  

Paper Submission at RuleML2012@ECAI

Authors are invited to submit original contributions1 of practical relevance and technical rigor in the field, experience reports and show case / use case demonstrations of effective, practical, deployable rule-based technologies or applications in distributed environments.

Papers must be in English and must be submitted at
http://www.easychair.org/conferences/?conf=ruleml2012
as:

  • Full Papers (15 pages in the proceedings)
  • Short Papers (8 pages in the proceedings)

If you have no EasyChair account, then you should sign up for an account.

Please upload all submissions as PDF files based on the LNCS format. To ensure high quality, submitted papers will be carefully peer-reviewed by at least 3 PC members based on originality, significance, technical soundness, and clarity of exposition.

Paper Submission at the 7th AIS SigPrag International Conference Track on Pragmatic Web (ICPW 2012)

Submissions to the 7th AIS SigPrag International Conference Track on Pragmatic Web (ICPW 2012) will be part of the RuleML 2012 submissions and should follow the RuleML2012@ECAI submission instructions. Additionally please add the keyword Pragmatic Web to your submission in easychair.

Paper Submission at RuleML2012@ECAI Doctoral Consortium

Thesis descriptions1 are limited to 8 pages in English using LNCS format and submitted electronically in PDF format jointly with a maximum 5 page CV.
Please submit to: http://www.easychair.org/conferences/?conf=ruleml2012consortium

Int. RuleML2012 Challenge Submissions

Please submit your contribution consisting of two parts:

  1. An open-source or commercial demo
  2. A demo paper of 4-8 pages and using LNCS format, describing research, implementation, and technical details of your submission.

Submit both the demo and the paper to http://www.easychair.org/conferences/?conf=ruleml2012challenge.
Would you have an online demo please indicate in the description paper an URL were it can be tested and evaluated.

1 Submitted manuscripts should not have been previously published in a journal or in the proceedings of a conference, and should not be under consideration for publication at another conference at any point during the RuleML2012@ECAI review process. It is acceptable to have a substantially extended version of the submitted paper under consideration simultaneously for journal publication, so long as the journal version's planned publication date is after August 2012 or later, the journal submission does not interfere with RuleML2012@ECAI right to publish the paper, and the situation is clearly described at the time of RuleML2012@ECAI submission.
